The model

A gate, then a ranking. Every number explained.

First a qualification gate with few, true disqualifiers — strike-off markers, corporate-group ownership, no live board. Then survivors are ranked by priority: succession exposure × data confidence, dampened (never deleted) by transaction complexity. The breakdown is the product — it survives contact with an investment committee.

Owner age35 pts

The controlling owner from the PSC register where one is named, else the oldest active director — ramping continuously from 55 to 75+. Month/year of birth only, shown as five-year bands. Age creates eligibility, never intent.

Urgency events35 pts

Behaviour, not demography: directors leaving without replacement, a fresh auditor or registered office, a shortened accounting period, security satisfied with none created. Events pick this month’s calls.

Control tenure10 pts

The longest-serving active director’s years at the table, from ten to twenty-five. Decades of unbroken control usually mean the founder still holds the pen.

Control concentration10 pts

Who actually owns it, from the PSC register — reported in bands, with trusts and protected records, so it is scored as a confidence, never a yes/no.

No visible successor10 pts

No director under 45 appointed in five years. A successor may exist off the register, so this signal is deliberately modest.

Transaction simplicity0–100

Starts at a hundred; ownership layers, crowded cap tables and charge churn subtract. Ordinary bank security costs nothing. Complexity dampens a target’s priority — it never deletes it.

Data confidence0–100

How much the register actually told us — owner age, ownership, filings, charges. Accounts are not yet ingested, so confidence tops out at 85 by construction: unknowns lower the number, they are never guessed.

Questions

The short answers

Where does the data come from?

Two official sources only: the Companies House bulk register snapshot (refreshed monthly) and the Companies House public API for officer records. Nothing scraped, nothing bought, nothing private.

Is this GDPR-compliant?

The pipeline processes public-register data under legitimate interest, stores director births as month/year only (exactly as Companies House publishes them), reports ages only as five-year bands, and every report names a removal-request contact. The methodology page states plainly what a score is and is not.

Can a score tell me an owner wants to sell?

No — and we print that on every report. A score measures similarity to typical pre-succession profiles from public filings. It tells you where a respectful, well-timed conversation is most likely to be worth having. The conversation is still yours to have.

What sectors can you cover?

Any UK sector definable by SIC codes. Logistics & freight (SIC 49–53) ships now, manufacturing is configured next, and a custom thesis takes about a day to set up before the standard five-day delivery.
