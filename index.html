Methodology

Six signals. One hundred points. No black box.

Every point in every score traces to a public filing at Companies House. The breakdown is the product — you can check our working, and you should.

Director age band · 40 pts

The strongest predictor of an exit is the calendar. Points scale linearly from the 55–60 band and peak where the eldest active director sits at 65 or beyond — month-and-year of birth as the register provides it, never a full date.

Sole or family control · 20 pts

A single active director, or a board sharing one surname, means the decision to sell belongs to one household. Concentrated ownership shortens every conversation that follows.

No heir signal · 15 pts

If no director under 45 has been appointed in five years, nobody is being groomed for the chair. The absence of a filing is itself a filing.

Company age · 10 pts

Points accrue from fifteen years of trading and peak at twenty-five. A business that has survived three recessions is a business someone will want to buy.

Stability · 10 pts

Still filing, on time, with no strike-off or insolvency events. We are pointing you at going concerns, not distressed paper.

Recency events · 5 pts

An auditor or registered-address change in the last eighteen months often means advisers have arrived. A small signal, weighted accordingly.
